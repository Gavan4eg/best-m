9 Helpful Ways To Stop Missing Your Ex

If you're missing your ex, it's essential to understand that it's a normal feeling and will take time to fade away. Allow yourself the space to heal, treating yourself with kindness like a friend going through a tough time. Embrace the pain and work through the grieving process. As you take care of yourself, process the breakup, and reflect on what happened, the intensity and duration of missing your ex will gradually decrease.

  • Over time, things around you won't constantly remind you of them, and you'll spend less time thinking about your ex. You'll wake up one day and realize you no longer love your ex. Eventually, you'll move on completely and stop missing them.
  • Alternatively, you might discover what you and your ex had is worth fighting for and decide to reconnect for another try.
  • In this article, we'll delve into why missing your ex is normal, what steps to process the breakup, how to cope and heal, and what to do if you're considering getting back together with your ex.

Why Do You Miss Your Ex?

If you find yourself missing your ex-boyfriend or ex-girlfriend after a breakup, it's a completely normal human response. Humans naturally miss their loved ones when they are not around.Missing an ex is a common part of the grief process following a breakup. The stronger your love for your ex, the more intense your feelings of missing them will likely be.

You may not want to miss your ex, as it can be painful to think about them. However, your mind and body operate based on their programming, shaped by millions of years of evolution.
Evolution has wired us to miss our exes when we go through a breakup and to long for the presence of someone we care about and love.

Humans are wired to follow daily rituals, resist change, and seek familiarity, comfort, and security. When these aspects are taken away, we experience a sense of loss.
Your ex provided familiarity, comfort, and a sense of security. The relationship assured you that you had someone by your side, creating a feeling of belonging together. The relationship became intertwined with your identity, shaping who you are.
